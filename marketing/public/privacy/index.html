A factual reference

Public means public.

Riot's current public communities are for information people intend to circulate. They are not private messengers, and Riot does not yet ship private encrypted groups.

Current public communities

Public means public

Current public Newswire content is plaintext, readable, and copyable by recipients and infrastructure that handles it. There is no confidential public-read boundary. Treat anything posted there as publishable.

Not shipped: private encrypted groups.

Pseudonymity is not anonymity. A signature proves control of a key, not a person's identity, truthfulness, or legitimacy.

Custody is not secrecy

What local-first changes—and what it does not

Participants can hold community state on their own devices, reducing the need to put every action through one centralized service. Already-held data may remain useful on a functioning device, and replaceable gateways can reduce dependence on one provider.

That does not hide copied public content, network metadata, IP addresses, timing, radio presence, or nearby activity. Gateways may present altered text; devices can be lost or compromised; observers can correlate behavior; and no design can recall every copy after publication.

The page you are reading

This website

Riot's static page code sets no cookies and includes no analytics, accounts, remote fonts, third-party scripts, tracking pixels, or fingerprinting. The pages work without JavaScript.

Hosting boundary: Cloudflare can observe ordinary request metadata and controls edge response headers. Inspecting these static files cannot guarantee what every independently configured hosting layer does.
